# University of Technology FAQ

## General Information
- The university campus is open from 6:00 AM to 11:00 PM, Monday through Sunday.
- For emergency, contact Campus Security at 555-0199.
- The academic year is divided into two main semesters: Fall (starts in September) and Spring (starts in February).

## Admissions
- The deadline for Fall undergraduate admissions is January 15th.
- International students require proof of English proficiency (TOEFL or IELTS).
- Application fees are $50 for domestic applicants and $75 for international applicants.

## Tuition and Financial Aid
- In-state undergraduate tuition is approximately $10,000 per academic year.
- Out-of-state undergraduate tuition is $28,000 per academic year.
- To apply for financial aid, students must submit the FAFSA by March 1st.
- Scholarships are awarded based on both merit and financial need. Check the portal for specific scholarships.

## Academics & Departments
- The Computer Science department is located in the Alan Turing Building.
- The minimum GPA required to remain in good academic standing is 2.0.
- A full-time undergraduate course load is considered 12 to 18 credits per semester.
- Dropping a course without a "W" grade is possible within the first two weeks of classes.

## Student Life
- Housing applications open on April 1st for the upcoming Fall semester.
- Meal plans are mandatory for freshmen living on campus.
- The Student Recreation Center offers a swimming pool, indoor track, and multiple gyms.
- Over 100 student clubs and organizations are available.

## Technology & Resources
- Campus-wide Wi-Fi is available via the 'UniversitySecure' network. Students must log in using their Student ID credentials.
- IT support is available 24/7 at support@university.edu or 555-0101.
- Libraries remain open 24 hours during final exam weeks.
